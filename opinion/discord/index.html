Reasons Not to Use Discord

(Yes, I know I use it)

1. Proprietary Software

The Discord communications system requires running a nonfree client program. That alone is reason to refuse to use it.

2. Data Collection & Ethics

The program reportedly collects lots of data about users. While the developer denies reporting which processes are running on your machine, selling personal data is not the sole way the company makes money.

What matters, ethically, is what the software does to users. The developers' motives are pertinent to understanding the situation but should not affect our moral judgment of the mistreatment of users.

3. Hostile Security Practices

Discord may demand to talk with you by phone if it decides you are suspicious—for instance, if you contact it via Tor.

Furthermore, the web app reportedly sends tracking data to /science instead of the usual /track to thwart privacy extensions. There is reportedly a switch to disable some collection, but it doesn't affect server-side tracking.

4. What to Use Instead

I would suggest a privacy-first alternative, such as:

  • Revolt
  • Matrix

5. Why do I still use it?

Good question. Well, because all of my friends are there. I can't switch all of my friends to Revolt or something lmao.
